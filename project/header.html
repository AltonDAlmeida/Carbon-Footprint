Why Track the Carbon Footprint of Coal Mines?

Coal mining is one of the most significant contributors to carbon emissions, which are directly linked to climate change. India, being one of the largest coal producers in the world, faces a critical challenge in balancing economic growth with environmental responsibility. By tracking the carbon footprint of coal mines, we can better understand the environmental cost of coal, drive policy changes, and support sustainable energy alternatives.

Overview of Coal Mining in India

History of Coal Mining: Coal mining in India began in the 1770s and today, the country is the second-largest coal producer in the world.

Major Coal Mining Regions: Coal reserves are primarily found in states like Jharkhand, West Bengal, Odisha, Chhattisgarh, and Madhya Pradesh.

Key Players in the Industry: Public sector company Coal India Limited dominates the industry, though private players are also emerging.

Environmental Footprint of Coal Mines

Deforestation and Land Degradation: Large areas of forest are cleared, causing loss of biodiversity and land degradation.

Air Pollution: Mining releases particulate matter and toxic gases like methane, leading to poor air quality.

Water Contamination: Acid mine drainage and heavy metals such as arsenic and lead pollute local water bodies.

Greenhouse Gas Emissions: Coal mining is a major contributor to India's carbon emissions.

Socioeconomic Impact of Coal Mining

While coal mining provides employment and boosts regional development, it also leads to the displacement of local and indigenous communities.

Government Initiatives and Sustainability Efforts

India is implementing regulatory frameworks and reclamation projects to mitigate the environmental damage caused by coal mining. There is also a growing focus on renewable energy.

Sustainable Alternatives & Future Goals

While coal remains a dominant energy source in India, there are growing efforts to transition to renewable energy like solar, wind, and hydroelectric power. Our goal is to support this transition by highlighting the long-term benefits of reducing coal dependency and investing in green technologies.
